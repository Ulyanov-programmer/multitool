Stylus

Stylus is the most progressive preprocessor for CSS. Fast, flexible, no extra syntax. You will find ready mixins.

See the Pen Power of Stylus by Ulyanov Ivan (@ulyanov-programmer) on CodePen.

Scripts (Typescript)

Like JS, only better. Prevents errors at the compilation stage, hints work fine in VS Code. Moreover, you will find many, MANY useful scripts here, and some of them you can watch below (don't be afraid, play with the settings).
Alas, Codepen does not allow me to show its advantages, but it should be enough for familiarization.

Swipe

See the Pen Swipe by Ulyanov Ivan (@ulyanov-programmer) on CodePen.

Animate by scroll

See the Pen Power Of Modules (animByScroll) by Ulyanov Ivan (@ulyanov-programmer) on CodePen.

Images

As usual - your PNG, JPG and others will be optimized, translated into webp... And avif. Yes, it's time to use it.

<img src="img/icons/img.png" alt='yourImg'> 
<picture>
 <source srcset="img/icons/img.avif" 
  type="image/avif">
 <source srcset="img/icons/img.webp" 
  type="image/webp">
 <img src="img/icons/img.png" 
  alt='yourImg'>
</picture>

Autoincluding the Swiper slider

The assembly automatically connects the Swiper slider to the project, its styles and scripts, if necessary.
This can be disabled when cleaning the build.

And of course - the required content.

Of course, you will find here such common things as: fileInclude, autoprefixer, optimization / cleaning styles and scripts (terser) and font converter (ttf2woff2). And something else that I forgot or considered not very necessary.
If you are interested in the packages used, look here.
